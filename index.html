Restriction enzyme sites across bacterial genomes - implications for metagenomics 6 Jan 2021 Restriction enzymes (REs) have been the workhorses of molecular biology for decades.1 Primarily considered tools of cloning, REs have found a place in some next-generation sequencing techniques that require efficient production of defined DNA ends for subsequent modification and ligation.
